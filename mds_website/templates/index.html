What is it?

The Muni Difficulty Scale is a collection of three scales which can be used to describe difficulties of trails for mountain unicycling.

There is already a large number of trail rating systems out there, however none of those fit the requirements for a scale that is applicable in the domain of mountain unicycling. This is why a group of mountain unicyclists started to develop the muni difficulty scale in 2012 which eventually became an official IUF standard in 2013.

M-Scale

The M-Scale is used to rate the difficulty of muni trail sections

It can be used individually from the other two scales to describe the technical difficulty of a trail, independently from its length or other attributes.

UDH-Scale

The Unicycle Downhill scale is used to rate the overall track difficulty of muni downhill tracks.

It takes technical difficulty as well as other factors into account.

UXC-Scale

The Unicycle Cross Country scale is used to rate the overall track difficulty of muni cross country tracks.

It takes technical difficulty as well as other factors into account.

A project by Rocco Schulz and the IUF. Design based on the Workless framework
